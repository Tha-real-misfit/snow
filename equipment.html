What type of clothing?


The most important rule for clothing, is to layer up in clothing that not only is waterproof but also can protect you.
You can find clothing ranging in prices and whilst the cheaper clothing might seem more appealing, it is recommended that you invest more upfront to save in the long run.
The basic set out that is recommended for boarders includes pants, jacket, neck warmer, helmet, gloves, goggles, and boots. There is then the inner wear which includes thermal clothing, pants, t-shirt, jumper, beanie, and warm thick socks.
When choosing outer wear, it is important to look at the waterproof (mm) and breathability (m2) levels. Both elements can be recognised through the number next to the item, e.g. 12,000 mm / m2. Basically, the higher the number the better level of waterproofing and breathability the item has.

These are some of the clothing options.

What type of board?


Choosing a board comes down to an individual’s weight and riding capabilities.
The length of a board will change in the situations you want to ride, for speed you want a longer board, for powder you want a board with a long nose and a short tail and for the park you will want an even length nose and tail, twin directional.
The width of the board should be slightly smaller than your boot. You want your boot to hang over each side of the board as this will help you control the board.

These are just a few options of board.

POWDER

With a longer nose and shorter tail, this board will help you cut through that deep powder.

POWDER SURF

With a longer nose and tail, this board will help you surf on top of any ungroomed trails.

PARK

With an equal nose and tail, this board will give you the ability to ride any groomer and hit up the park.
